Earthquakes

Comprehensive Guide: Precautions and Preparedness

Overview


An earthquake is a sudden and violent shaking of the ground, often caused by movement along fault lines or volcanic activity. Earthquakes can cause damage to buildings, roads, and other infrastructure, and can trigger secondary hazards such as tsunamis, landslides, and fires.

Precautions - Before and After an Earthquake


Do's Before an Earthquake

Secure Items:

Ensure heavy furniture and objects are securely anchored to the wall to prevent them from falling during the earthquake.

Establish Safe Rooms:

Designate and practice safe spots, like under tables or against interior walls, to take shelter in case of shaking.

Emergency Kit:

Prepare an emergency kit with essentials like water, non-perishable food, first-aid supplies, and a flashlight.

Do's During an Earthquake

Find a safe Place to hide:

Try to find a safe place like under the table and try to hid in elevators or try to run out of buildings.

Do's After an Earthquake

Check for Damages:

Inspect your home for structural damage and turn off gas lines to prevent fire hazards.

Stay Away from Damaged Areas:

Avoid damaged buildings and infrastructure to reduce the risk of injury from aftershocks or collapsing structures.

Assist Others:

Help injured individuals and those in need, particularly elderly and children.

Preparedness


  • Develop a family emergency plan and practice evacuation drills.
  • Stay connected to community warning systems for real-time alerts.
  • Strengthen buildings to withstand natural disasters.
  • Learn basic first-aid techniques and CPR to assist during emergencies.
  • Establish a communication plan to contact loved ones during a disaster.
