Floods

Comprehensive Guide: Causes, Effects, and Preparedness

Overview


Flooding occurs when water overflows onto normally dry land, often due to heavy rainfall, overflowing rivers, or storm surges. Floods can cause widespread destruction to property, disrupt transportation, and lead to loss of life. It is essential to prepare for floods to minimize their impact.

Precautions - Before and After a Flood


Do's Before a Flood

Prepare Sandbags:

Use sandbags to block water from entering your home.

Move Valuables to Higher Levels:

Store important items and documents on higher floors or safe locations.

Stay Updated:

Keep track of weather reports and flood warnings from local authorities.

Do's After a Flood

Check for Contamination:

Ensure your drinking water is safe before consumption.

Avoid Standing Water:

Standing water can hide hazards such as debris and contamination.

Report Damages:

Notify local authorities of structural damages or other flood-related issues.

Preparedness


  • Know your flood risk and understand flood warnings.
  • Develop an emergency plan and evacuation routes.
  • Keep essential supplies, including water, food, and medications, ready.
  • Install water-resistant barriers and backflow valves in your home.
  • Stay informed with emergency alerts and notifications.
