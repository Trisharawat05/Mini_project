Hurricanes

Comprehensive Guide: Causes, Effects, and Preparedness

Overview


Hurricanes are large, swirling storms characterized by strong winds and heavy rains. Forming over warm ocean waters, they can cause massive damage to coastal areas through storm surges, flooding, and extreme wind speeds. Preparedness is key to minimizing their devastating impact.

Precautions - Before and After a Hurricane


Do's Before a Hurricane

Secure Your Home:

Install storm shutters and reinforce your roof and garage doors to minimize damage.

Evacuation Plan:

Identify evacuation routes and shelters in your area, and keep your vehicle fueled.

Emergency Supplies:

Assemble a hurricane kit with water, food, batteries, flashlights, and first-aid items.

Do's After a Hurricane

Stay Informed:

Continue monitoring updates from local authorities about safety and recovery efforts.

Avoid Floodwaters:

Stay away from standing water as it may be contaminated or hide hazards.

Inspect for Damages:

Carefully check your home for structural damages and report any hazards to authorities.

Preparedness


  • Understand hurricane categories and warnings.
  • Regularly inspect your property for vulnerabilities to strong winds and rain.
  • Create a communication plan with family members.
  • Ensure you have flood insurance coverage.
